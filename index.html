STEPS Decision Aid Tool

Scaling Field Epidemiology Training Programs (FETP) in India is crucial for enhancing public health surveillance, outbreak response, and data-driven decision-making. This tool utilizes hypothetical estimates based on an error component logit model to predict program uptake probabilities and conduct cost-benefit analyses, enabling informed decisions on program scaling based on key attributes.

Use the tabs above to navigate:

  • Instructions – Guidance for each tool feature.
  • Attributes – Detailed descriptions of the tool attributes.
  • Inputs – Configure your training scenario’s parameters.
  • Predicted Uptake – View charts predicting training uptake.
  • Costs & Benefits – Analyze the financial implications of scenarios.
  • Scenarios – Save, compare, or export multiple training configurations.

How to Use This Tool

  1. Review the Attributes to understand the key factors influencing training program scaling.
  2. Navigate to the Inputs tab to configure your desired training scenario by selecting different attribute levels.
  3. After setting your inputs, switch to the Predicted Uptake tab to view the predicted training uptake based on your configuration.
  4. Check the Costs & Benefits tab to analyze the financial implications of your scenario.
  5. Use the Scenarios tab to save your configurations, compare multiple scenarios, or export your results to PDF for reporting.

Tool Attributes

Attribute Levels Description
Training Level Frontline, Intermediate, Advanced The complexity and depth of the training program.
Delivery Method In-Person, Online, Hybrid The mode through which training is delivered to participants.
Accreditation National, International, None The formal recognition of the training program by authoritative bodies.
Location of Training District-Level, State-Level, Regional Centers The geographical setting where training is conducted.
Cohort Size Small (10-20), Medium (21-50), Large (51+) The number of participants in each training batch.
Cost per Participant Continuous (₹100,000 - ₹600,000) The financial investment required per trainee.

Cost-Benefit Analysis

Cost Components

  • Advertisement: ₹34,990.60 (Includes advertisements in local media and online platforms)
  • Training Materials: ₹50,000 (Includes manuals, digital resources, and equipment)
  • Trainer Salaries: ₹150,000 (Compensation for trainers conducting sessions)
  • Venue Hire: ₹40,000 (Cost of renting training facilities)
  • Participant Support: ₹30,000 (Includes transportation and accommodation for participants)
  • Administrative Costs: ₹25,000 (Includes project management and administrative support)

Benefits Measurement

Benefits are measured in terms of improved public health outcomes, quantified as Quality-Adjusted Life Years (QALYs). Each participant gains an estimated 0.05 QALYs through enhanced epidemiological skills, leading to better disease surveillance and outbreak response.
